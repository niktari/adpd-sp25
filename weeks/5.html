  • Technical Exercise: Nested Targeting & Hovering Worksheet

    Complete the Nested Targeting & Hovering Worksheet. Make sure to save your own version of the worksheet in your CodePen account (click on the "Save" button or hit Command + S) to start editing it. Be sure to save your work along the way. You are permitted to work with other classmates on this worksheet, but you must submit your own CodePen link.

    Don’t worry if you don’t know how to answer all of the questions, just do the best you can! This assignment is solely graded based on completion. If you don’t know the answer, note that in your worksheet. This will help us understand which concepts need more reinforcement.

    To get full credit, you must hit all of the requirements below.

  • Submit a Website: Poster

    Submit 1-2 websites that you think are poster-like to the Teams discussion post. Describe in your post why you think it resembles a poster. Is it the use of typography? Of scale? Of space? Is there a central focal point? Does it communicate information effectively?

  • Download Starter Code

    For Project 2, I’ve created starter code for you that will serve as the basis of your project files. Be sure to download the starter code, unzip it, and drag it into your username.github.io (root) folder. It should have the same level of hierarchy as your project-1 folder.

  • Poster, Part 1: Concept & Sketch

    Using one HTML file and three different stylesheets, create three completely different versions of your poster to explore initial concepts. Yes, that means you’ll switch between styles by changing the linked stylesheet in your HTML for each version! If it's helpful, feel free to plan your designs in Figma before bringing them into code.

    Switch between the stylesheets using your <link> tag.

    The requirements for each version are below:

    1. Version 1: Design one within a "poster shape," aka vertical dimensions.

    2. Version 2: Design one that uses the full width and height of the browser and uses only typography.

    3. Version 3: Design one that's ugly. You decide what that entails.

    This assignment is worth 4 out of 8 points of Project 2: Poster. To get full credit, you must hit all of the requirements below.
