Assignments

  • Technical Exercise: CSS Layout Worksheet

    Complete the CSS Layout Worksheet. Make sure to save your own version of the worksheet in your CodePen account (click on the "Save" button or hit Command + S) to start editing it. Be sure to save your work along the way. You are permitted to work with other classmates on this worksheet, but you must submit your own CodePen link.

    Don’t worry if you don’t know how to answer all of the questions, just do the best you can! This assignment is solely graded based on completion. If you don’t know the answer, note that in your worksheet. This will help us understand which concepts need more reinforcement.

    To get full credit, you must hit all of the requirements below.

  • Book, Part 3: Finalize

    Continue refining your project based on feedback, focusing on the typographic system you’re developing. Are you applying consistent styles to headings, paragraphs, and other text elements? Ensure your design choices support your intended user experience and evoke the themes of your text. If it makes sense for your project, see if you can experiment with the layout more and break the top-to-bottom, left-to-right nature of the webpage. To do this, perhaps you’d like to implement what you now know about the CSS box model and positioning properties.

    We will conduct our critiques in FigJam, Figma’s collaborative whiteboarding tool. You will present your project from your own laptop, while your classmates follow along using a direct link to your website.

    You'll have about 5-7 minutes to present, with 2-3 minutes for feedback from your classmates. I recommend you prepare your talking points beforehand so that you can speak to your work clearly yet succinctly.

    Before next week’s class, make sure to:

    1. Paste a link to your project in your assigned crit board in this FigJam document. (Required)
    2. Add screenshots or reference images that supplement your presentation. (Optional)

    Ignore the crit questions and sticky notes for now—we’ll introduce them during class next week.

    This assignment is worth 4 out of 20 points of Project 1: Book. To get full credit, you must hit all of the requirements below.
