Description

This week, we will expand on our previous lesson by learning more about functions in JavaScript and why it’s important to use them. Just like how a variable saves data, a function saves an action. Moving beyond HTML inputs, we’ll also learn how JavaScript can listen for specific user interactions, such as mouse movement and keyboard input.

  • Generative Tool, Part 6: Polish & Present

    Continue to refine your tool so its functionality is nailed down. Polish any remaining design elements: layout, typography, visual language, etc. See if you can make your tool responsive across different devices (revisit fluid units and media queries)!

    Test your tool with at least three users. It might be helpful to ask each user to complete a specific task with your tool. Evaluate how they interact with your tool and gather any feedback and insights. Adjust your tool based on any notable feedback you gathered from your users.

    Develop a presentation for Project 2 that walks us through your process and idea. You will have 3 minutes to present, so think about how you can give us a succinct yet thorough walkthrough of your work. Make sure you cover the following points in your presentation:

    1. Content and Approach: Introduce the your concept and the main purpose of your tool. Discuss your approach to developing it.
    2. Tool: Show us your live tool. Walk us through the inputs and outputs. What type of HTML inputs (e.g. button, range, color) did you use? What type of JavaScript events (onclick, onkeydown, onmouseover) did you use? Why did you decide to use these inputs and events? Talk about the design decisions you made, and how they contribute to the concept and functionality of your tool.
    3. User Testing: Talk about your user testing, and highlight any notable insights and feedback you gathered. Did you change any aspects of your tool based on user feedback?
