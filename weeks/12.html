Assignments

  • Clock, Part 1: Concept & Sketch

    Decide on your clock’s purpose (if any). Does it prompt specific actions, like making a wish at 11:11? Does it visualize daily habits, like watering your plants? Or is it purely abstract?

    Once you’ve determined your clock’s functionality, sketch out 10-15 distinct ideas using pen and paper. Explore far and wide! This is an exercise in pushing yourself creatively. At least one sketch should be purely typographic and one should be purely illustrative. Digitize two of your concepts in Figma (or any digital tool of your choice) to prototype how your clock will work and be interacted with. If your clock has a specific use case, what are the relevant time intervals, and how are they displayed? For instance, what does your clock look like when it’s 11:11? When it’s not 11:11? Sketch both of those instances.

    If you feel like getting ahead, start to translate your winning idea into code.

    This assignment is worth 5 out of 25 points of Project 3: Clock. To get full credit, you must hit all of the requirements below.
