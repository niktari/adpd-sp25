  • Book, Part 3: Style with CSS

    Bring a portion of your text in Figma and test at least three different typographic palettes before implementing them in CSS.

    Choose one typographic palette and apply it to your content using CSS. Begin with basic typographic properties such as font-family, font size, line-height, and letter-spacing. Explore additional CSS properties to elevate the design and usability of your website.

    Consider how certain typefaces can influence the legibility and tone of the content. How do you think the author would have intended the text to be read? Do your typographic choices enhance or disrupt the experience?

    You will have one stylesheet for all of your pages. Make sure you create a CSS file named styles.css file in your project-1 subfolder. Double check your folder structure in your Finder view to ensure that this stylesheet is actually under the project-1 subfolder and not your username.github.io folder.

    Link to that stylesheet in every page of your book using the <link> tag. (Yes, that means every HTML page!) It should be placed under your <title> tag in your <head> section. The tag should look something like:

    <link href=”styles.css” rel=”stylesheet”>

    Think about how you’re developing an overall design system for your book: based on your typographic palette, how are headings, subheadings, paragraphs styled? If you need to give a subheading a certain styling, consider giving it a class or an ID.
